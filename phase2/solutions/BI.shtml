Business Intelligience

A required infrastructure to support Corporate Performance Management, Business Intelligence is the ability to aggregate, analyze, report and add value to production data created in transaction systems.

The mandate to leverage growing data stores to run the business more effectively is spreading from the department into the enterprise, growing the addressable market for BI.

We at Ei Partners realize that there are disparate and independent processes designed to collect, monitor, analyze and report corporate information.

Unified Business View. Synchronize information centrally from all systems throughout your enterprise for an accurate business view. Right Time Intelligence. Enable the right person to get the right information at the right time and quickly respond to changes in your business environment.

Due to this fact, Cognos recommends an iterative approach to Business Intelligence Application Development. Generally scoped to three or four iterations, the first phases spend the a considerable time with requirements gathering, and little time with development, but by completion of phase one, the end users are able to interact with the data. Each iteration will gather new requirements spending more and more time with development as the project progresses. The final phase will include steps to productionalize the environment.
The introduction of Sarbanes-Oxley, Basel II, and the Patriot Act have made access to and analysis of information critical. Due to a diminished economic environment over the past few years, Business Intelligence has been increasingly used to understand and address back office needs such as efficiency and productivity
Within the Cognos methodology, Cognos initially gathers requirements around five Information Quality Metrics (Content, Accuracy, Timeliness, Functionality and Coordination) that establish a baseline against which business requirements can be defined and technical solutions evaluated. The baseline requirements gathering will help guide the technical architecture as well, as information timeliness will drastically impact the direction to take as whether to move certain items in batch or though more real time methods. Understanding the functionality and coordination will help drive the end user environment and the BI modelling (understanding which tool will best represent which data), while defining requirements based upon content, can help prioritize which subject areas targeted for delivery in various iterations
